#1 What conditions drove Europeans to explore west across the Atlantic? Early explorers like Christopher Columbus were searching for a sea route to the east Indies, which was a major source of trade for European empires.

New technologies such as the mariner's compass and the astrolabe as well as the invention of the caravel made it possible for wealthy nations such as Spain to fund voyages west.
#2 Conquistadores Spanish soldiers who conquered almost all of the Caribbean, Central America, and South America.

They killed and displaced native populations while spreading Spanish language and religion across America, providing the foundation for Latin American colonies and nations that would later arise there.

The gold they received from Native Americans brought great wealth to Europe and encouraged continued exploitation of indigenous populations.
#3 Encomienda Spanish policy that allowed Caribbean colonists to "adopt" Native Americans to use them for labour and convert them to Christianity.

First indications of the slave trade that would become essential to Caribbean plantations
#4 Treaty of Tortesillas Divided American lands between Portugal and Spain, with Portugal receiving a small portion of coastal land that would later become Brazil.
#5 How did the Columbian exchange change American and European life? The conquistadores' plundering of American gold and luxury goods brought huge wealth to Spain and helped to develop capitalism in Europe.

Horses and livestock brought to America by Europeans revolutionized Native American way of life, particularly on the Great Plains, where horses were used to great effect.

New crops such as corn, potatoes, beans, and tomatoes allowed Europeans to sustain large urban growth well into the 18th century.

Diseases like smallpox and scarlet fever decimated Native American populations, allowing colonists to easily displace them.
#6 How did England become involved in the New World? England's protestant revolution kept it out of colonial affairs during Spain's conquest, but in the late 1500's, it emerged as wealthy, unified power like Spain had been at the start of the previous century.

The defeat of the Spanish armada by English sea-dogs in 1588 left the Atlantic seaway open for British ships.

English primogeniture, which allowed only the oldest sons of landowners to inherit land, and the enclosure of poor farmers by larger operations created a source of poor servants and debtors who were eager for a new life.

New wealth seeping from Spain and the emergence of the Joint-stock company provided funds for risky colonial undertakings in America.
#7 What saved Jamestown from destruction during its early years? John Smith's leadership forced colonists to gather resources and plant crops rather than search for gold.

Tobacco cultivation made Jamestown extremely profitable, especially after the introduction of slavery by the Dutch.
#8 House of Burgesses Jamestown governing body that used a parliamentary system of independent representation.

First inkling of democratic government to emerge in America.
#9 Act of Toleration Passed by Maryland representative assembly to guarantee freedom of worship to all Christians.

Allowed Maryland to become a safe haven for Catholics from the surrounding protestant colonies.

First indication of Freedom of religion in America.
#10 What factors led to the founding of New England colonies? The protestant revolution in England left a hastily converted Church of England in power that many viewed as corrupt, so some chose to become separatists and establish a new protestant society in America

Notable separatists included the Pilgrims who settled at Plymouth and the Puritans who settled the Massachusetts Bay Colony.

All northern colonies emerged as offshoots of the Massachusetts Bay Colony, including Rhode Island, which originated as a haven for political dissenters like Anne Hutchinson and Roger Williams who questioned Congregationalism and the role of government in religious matters.
#11 John Winthrop Leader of the Massachusetts Bay Colony.

Delivered the "city upon a hill" speech that proclaimed the idea of America as a model of prosperity for the rest of the world.
#12 What were early colonial relations with Native Americans like? After being forced by hunger to raid Native American camps for food, Jamestown settlers under command of Lord De La Warr used "Irish tactics" to remove Native Americans from the Chesapeake during the first Anglo-Powhatan war.

After attacks on several northern Carolina settlements, Carolinians crushed Native Americans in the Tuscarora War.

New Englanders virtually annihilated the Pequot tribe in the Pequot war after tensions burst due to colonial expansion.
#13 What led to the rise of slavery in colonial America? Indentured servants originally made up a significant portion of colonists in the Chesapeake region, but they were difficult to control, as proved by Bacon's rebellion against Virginia landowners.

African slaves, first brought to America by Dutch merchants, were well suited to tobacco and rice cultivation in the swampy Chesapeake region due to their natural resistance to malaria

Slaves proved easier to control than indentured servants as "slave codes" allowed for extreme punishments for minor infractions.

Slave rebellions only occasionally flared up, notably the New York slave revolt of 1712 and the Stono River rebellion
